The object of the game is to auction any of the cards in your hand you don't want, decieve your buyer, and blind bid on items based on their value. The player with the most points in their hand at the end wins. Throw out your moral compass, because in this game, it's okay to lie.

Game Play
To begin a round, the dealer will flip the hourglass to start the time. Looking at your hand, decide which cards you want to lock or auction by looking at the number of points listed on each card, e.g. lock cards with 60-100 points and auction 0-40.

The Buyer
To bid on an item card, buyers ask sellers questions to deduct if the information has value. Buyers use chips to purchase cards and CANNOT trade their item cards for auction items. Once the seller says "Sold", buyers place the payment in the pot for the house to collect. If you don't auction during the round, at the end of the round, any remaining chips are deducted from your point total.

Why?
Society loves celebrities but instead of defining the problem, we laugh it off because, at the end of the day, how much does it affect our lives? Celebrities are equally good and bad. Though there is an understanding that celebrities lives are unrealistic, we'll love them until one day they're being cancelled on Twitter.
    The project was created to encourage users ‚Äî like yourself ‚Äî to take an honest accounting on how you choose to spend your time. From buying something on Instagram you were baited into, choosing which news source to believe, and cycling through the same TikTok trend; these decisions are ultimately manipulated by an algorithm. It's difficult to do even when being alert about these issues. However, the consequence is believing everything we see online is the truth; and because of this, your perception can be altered.
