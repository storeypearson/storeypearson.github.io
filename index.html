Introducing our first card game Phantom’s Bazaar!
Have you ever wondered what happened to Kanye’s bag of air or how you’d book a dance lesson with Matthew Morrison for your enemies? Neither have we — but in this game, you can own one of you favorite celebrities possessions at the low LOW cost of your dignity.

How to Play
Imagine you’re rich enough to be able to afford a celebrities’ dirty and used items. If you can't, don't love that for you...
The object of the game is to auction any of the cards in your hand you don’t want, decieve your buyer, and blind bid on items based on their value. The player with the most points in their hand at the end of the game wins. Unlike most things, in this game it’s okay to lie.

Game Play
To begin a round, the dealer will flip the hourglass to start the time. Looking at your hand, decide which cards you want to lock or auction by looking at the number of points listed on each card, e.g. lock cards with 60-100 points and auction 0-40.

03


The Buyer
To bid on an item card, buyers ask sellers questions to deduct if the information has value. Buyers use chips to purchase cards and CANNOT trade their item cards for auction items. Once the seller says "Sold", buyers place the payment in the pot for the house to collect. If you don't auction during the round, at the end of the round, any remaining chips are deducted from your point total.

The Seller
To auction an item card, place the card in your card holder with the item side facing you (shown in the picture below). This indicates that the item is up for auction. Once doing so, you cannot take back the card. Sellers can describe the item but cannot include words in the title. E.g. when describing the object, ‘John Lennon’s Tooth’ you can say something like, “It was sculpted by God”. When the item is sold, the seller must yell “Sold” to indicate the auction period has ended. Until then, the auction remains open.

About

Who?
Phantom's Bazaar is a project designed by Storey Pearson for the Communication Design thesis course at Parsons School of Design.

It’s designed for young adults who are susceptible to aimless scrolling, and technology-minded adults.

The Phantom's Bazaar PDF is open to everyone at no cost. You can access the link here at any time.

However you made it, thanks for participating.

Why?
Society loves celebrities but instead of defining the problem, we laugh it off because, at the end of the day, how much does it affect our lives? Though there is an understanding that celebrities lives are unrealistic, many people buy into the world. Even though celebrities will always exist, the project was created to encourage users to take an honest accounting on how they choose to spend their time. From buying something on Instagram you were baited into, choosing which news source to believe, and cycling through the same TikTok trend; these decisions are ultimately manipulated by an algorithm. Without doing so, the consequence is believing everything we see online is the truth, and our perceptions and opinions can be altered by algorithms.
